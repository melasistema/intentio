# Lean Canvas Framework

The Lean Canvas is a 1-page business model template designed for rapid iteration and actionable business planning. Created by Ash Maurya, it adapts the Business Model Canvas for startups and entrepreneurs.

## The 9 Building Blocks

### 1. Problem (Top 3)
List the top 3 problems your customers face.
- Focus on real pain points, not assumed needs
- Rank by severity and frequency
- Keep it specific and observable

### 2. Customer Segments
Who are your target customers?
- Define narrow, specific groups initially
- Identify early adopters first
- Consider demographics, behaviors, and needs
- Example: "Busy urban professionals aged 25-40"

### 3. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
A clear, compelling statement of why someone should buy from you.
- Answer: "Why you, why now?"
- Must be specific and differentiated
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Template: "We help [customer] achieve [benefit] by [unique approach]"

### 4. Solution
How do you solve the problems?
- List top 3 features that address each problem
- Keep it minimal (MVP mindset)
- Focus on solving the problem, not building features

### 5. Channels
How will you reach your customers?
- Consider: Direct sales, online marketing, partnerships, content, etc.
- Distinguish between free vs. paid channels
- Think about customer acquisition path

### 6. Revenue Streams
How will you make money?
- Pricing model: subscription, one-time, freemium, etc.
- Customer lifetime value (LTV) projection
- Revenue = (# customers) × (price) × (frequency)

### 7. Cost Structure
What are your major costs?
- Fixed costs (salaries, rent, infrastructure)
- Variable costs (per customer/transaction)
- Focus on biggest cost drivers
- Calculate burn rate and runway

### 8. Key Metrics
What numbers will you track to measure success?
- Pick 1-3 critical metrics (not vanity metrics)
- Examples: MRR, churn rate, CAC, activation rate
- These should drive decisions

### 9. Unfair Advantage
What can't be easily copied or bought?
- This is the hardest box to fill (and that's okay early on)
- Examples: Network effects, insider information, dream team, existing customers
- If empty, focus on building one over time

## Using Lean Canvas Effectively

**Start with Problem and Customer Segments**: These are your riskiest assumptions.

**Iterate regularly**: The canvas should evolve as you learn.

**Keep it to 1 page**: Forces clarity and prioritization.

**Test assumptions**: Each box contains hypotheses—validate them with real data.

## Common Mistakes

❌ Being too broad with customer segments  
❌ Focusing on features instead of problems  
❌ Claiming things are "unfair advantages" that aren't  
❌ Making revenue projections without validation  
❌ Treating it as a static document  

The Lean Canvas is not a business plan. It's a thinking tool for systematic experimentation.
